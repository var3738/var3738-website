REPORT ON GROUND ENGAGEMENTS IN TRANS NZOIA COUNTY
Preparatory Civic Engagement for the “From the Streets to the Ballot” Initiative Prepared
by: The Viable Alternative Republic (VAR)

1. Background
The Viable Alternative Republic (VAR) is undertaking a civic education initiative aimed at
translating the civic energy witnessed during the recent Gen Z protests into structured
democratic participation. The initiative is guided by VAR’s civic mobilization slogan:
“From the Streets to the Ballot.”
The objective of this campaign is to encourage young people and communities to move beyond
protest-based civic engagement and actively participate in democratic processes such as voter
registration, civic oversight, and elections.
As part of preparations for a major civic education event scheduled at the end March, VAR
deployed a team to conduct preliminary ground engagements in Trans Nzoia County. The
purpose of the exercise was to:


Listen directly to community concerns;



Assess public attitudes toward electoral institutions and democratic participation;



Identify socio-economic and governance issues affecting voter engagement;



Inform the design and messaging of the upcoming civic education event.

The engagements were conducted through brief public conversations, informal dialogues, and
community interactions across several wards.

2. Scope of the Engagement
The VAR team conducted engagements across 12 wards within 4 constituencies in Trans Nzoia
County. The discussions involved:
•

youth groups

•

traders

•

boda boda operators

•

farmers

•

students

•

community leaders

•

informal sector workers

These conversations provided a cross-sectional perspective on local governance, service
delivery, electoral participation, and socio-economic challenges affecting the residents.
While the discussions were informal, they revealed clear patterns of common concerns across
the county, alongside unique ward-specific issues that reflect local governance realities.

3. Common Issues Identified Across the County
Across all wards visited, the VAR team observed a set of recurring concerns that residents
consistently raised. These issues represent structural challenges affecting governance,
economic stability, and civic participation.
3.1 Declining Public Trust in the Electoral System
One of the most frequently raised issues was the loss of public confidence in the electoral
management system, particularly the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission
(IEBC).
Residents expressed concerns about:
•

perceived corruption within electoral institutions;

•

manipulation of electoral outcomes;

•

intimidation during election periods;

•

lack of accountability for electoral malpractice.

This perception has created significant voter apathy, especially among youth. Many participants
indicated that they believe elections do not accurately reflect the will of the people, which
discourages participation.
This issue is particularly important in the context of VAR’s initiative because the success of the
“From the Streets to the Ballot” campaign depends heavily on restoring public trust in
democratic processes.
3.2 Unemployment and Youth Economic Exclusion
Unemployment emerged as a major driver of social instability across the county. Residents
highlighted that:
•

many young people are unable to secure stable employment;

•

available jobs within the county are often inaccessible to locals;

•

informal work provides insufficient income for basic needs.

As a result, unemployment has contributed to:
•

increased crime rates;

•

recruitment of youth into political violence;

•

drug and alcohol abuse;

•

growing insecurity in certain areas.

Community members emphasized that economic desperation often makes young people
vulnerable to manipulation by political actors, particularly during election cycles.
3.3 Limited Access to Voter Registration
Participants consistently reported that voter registration services are not easily accessible,
especially in remote parts of Trans Nzoia County.
Residents reported that:
•

voter registration centers are located far from rural communities;

•

registration exercises are not adequately publicized;

•

mobile registration services rarely reach remote wards.

This lack of accessibility effectively excludes many eligible voters, particularly youth and rural
residents, from participating in elections.
3.4 Challenges in Obtaining National Identification Documents
The process of obtaining or replacing national identification cards was widely criticized.
Residents reported several barriers, including:
•

fees associated with acquiring or replacing IDs;

•

long delays in ID processing;

•

bureaucratic errors that require repeated corrections;

•

alleged deliberate mistakes that prolong the process.

Some participants also raised concerns about cross-border voter irregularities, particularly
allegations that individuals from Uganda are sometimes able to participate in Kenyan elections.
Whether verified or not, such perceptions significantly affect public trust in electoral integrity.
3.5 Poor Infrastructure and Public Service Delivery
Residents across multiple wards highlighted severe deficiencies in public infrastructure and
services, including:
•

inadequate hospitals and health facilities;

•

poor road networks;

•

lack of security lighting;

•

poorly developed markets;

•

limited access to clean water;

•

unreliable electricity supply;

•

insufficient police posts;

•

absence of public sanitation facilities.

These infrastructure deficits contribute to economic stagnation and insecurity, particularly in
rural areas.
3.6 Ineffective Public Participation in Governance
Participants reported that public participation processes required by law are either not
conducted or not taken seriously.
Residents indicated that:
•

consultations on county development plans are rarely held;

•

when meetings occur, community input is rarely reflected in final decisions;

•

public participation is sometimes conducted merely to satisfy procedural requirements.

This has led to a perception that citizens have little influence over development priorities and
resource allocation.
3.7 Tribalism and Discrimination in Access to Services
Despite Trans Nzoia being a cosmopolitan county, tribal identity was frequently cited as a factor
affecting access to opportunities.
Residents reported instances where:
•

certain communities receive preferential treatment in public services;

•

employment opportunities are influenced by ethnic identity;

•

access to documents such as IDs may be affected by tribal considerations.

This has contributed to social divisions and feelings of marginalization among some groups.

3.8 Rising Insecurity
Insecurity was a widely reported concern.
Residents cited issues such as:
•

theft and robbery;

•

youth involvement in crime;

•

drug abuse;

•

gang activity in some urban areas.

Many participants expressed concern that economic hardship and political manipulation of
unemployed youth are worsening security conditions.
4. Ward-Specific Issues
In addition to the countywide concerns, several unique issues emerged in specific wards.
4.1 Endebess Ward
Residents raised concerns regarding:
•

perceived institutional weaknesses within national cohesion mechanisms-specifically
The National Cohesion and Integration Commission (NCIC);

•

alleged interference by police in the operations of commercial motorcycle transport
operators (boda bodas);

•

limited access to affordable credit and financial services;

lack of employment opportunities despite the presence of several companies operating
in the county.
Community members questioned why local populations do not benefit from investments made
within the region.

5. Implications for Civic Engagement
The findings from these engagements highlight a critical intersection between governance
challenges and democratic participation.
Key implications include:


declining trust in electoral institutions;



widespread socio-economic frustration;



youth disillusionment with political processes;



structural barriers to voter registration and participation.

These conditions help explain the strong civic mobilization seen during recent youth-led protests
and underscore the importance of converting protest energy into constructive democratic
engagement.

6. Conclusion
The engagements conducted by VAR across Trans Nzoia County reveal a population that remains
deeply concerned about governance, economic opportunity, and electoral integrity.
While frustrations are widespread, residents also demonstrated a strong desire for meaningful
participation in shaping their future.
The upcoming VAR civic education initiative therefore presents an important opportunity to:


rebuild public confidence in democratic processes;



empower citizens with knowledge about their rights and responsibilities;



encourage constructive political participation.

Through initiatives such as “From the Streets to the Ballot,” VAR seeks to contribute to a
democratic culture where civic action translates into informed electoral participation and
accountable governance.
